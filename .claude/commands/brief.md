You are producing my daily brief. Read these files first, in order:

1. `CLAUDE.md` for identity and voice rules
2. `context/priorities.md` for what is on my plate
3. `context/operating_principles.md` for how I want my week to feel
4. `memory/decisions.md` for recent commitments (last 14 days)
5. `memory/relationships.md` for any open threads with people

Then produce the brief.

## Output format

Paragraph form only. No bullet points. No headers. Three short paragraphs, in this order:

1. **What is on my plate today.** The actual work I should be doing, named specifically. Not a calendar dump. The 2-4 things that matter, framed in a way that reminds me why they matter.

2. **What is slipping.** Anything that has been on `priorities.md` or `decisions.md` for more than its expected window without movement. Be direct. If I am procrastinating on something, say so. If I am letting a relationship go cold, say so.

3. **The one thing I should not forget.** A single sentence. The thing I am most likely to drop today that I would regret dropping. Often a person, a follow-up, or a small commitment that is about to expire.

## Voice constraints

No em dashes ever. No AI tells (no "delve," "navigate," "it is not just X, it is Y," "leverage" as a verb, "unlock"). First-person analytical voice. Direct prose. Do not flatter me. If today looks light, say it looks light, do not invent urgency.

If `context/priorities.md` is mostly empty placeholders, say that explicitly and stop. Do not hallucinate priorities.
